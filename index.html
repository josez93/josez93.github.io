Who Am I?

Growing up I always knew I wanted to do something related to technology. I began my tech journey studying to become a help desk professional. I tried to learn it by myself but I found it to be very difficult, so I looked for different options and stumbled upon Actualize Coding Bootcamp. It became clear that software development was an obvious choice, I like making stuff and I like tech.

Coding in the back end, I enjoy having knowledge of what's going on. When I started coding in September 2020 I felt like seeing the gears inside of a clock. It's always nice to look at a very complex system, but you don't always know what each gear is for. Now that I have more in-depth knowledge than when I started, I feel pretty comfortable knowing what each gear is for--metaphorically not literally, I still don't know what each gear on a clock does. Front end coding is more fun. I like being able to see the visual changes on the web page more often as I make them. And, users who have no coding knowledge can also give constructive criticism on the visual elements of the page. But constructive criticism isn't all that bad it helps you know what to improve on and forces one to keep learning.
